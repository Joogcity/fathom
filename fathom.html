Understanding Carbon Credits

What Are Carbon Credits?

A carbon credit represents the verified removal or prevention of one tonne of CO₂ equivalent from entering the atmosphere. When your business purchases and retires a credit, you can legitimately claim an offset against that tonne of emissions in your own footprint.

Credits are generated by projects that either capture carbon — like reforestation or soil sequestration — or prevent emissions that would otherwise occur, such as protecting threatened forests or replacing fossil fuels with renewables. Each project is independently audited and verified against internationally recognised standards before any credits are issued.

Retirement is the critical step: once a credit is retired in a public registry on your behalf, it can never be sold or used again — ensuring your offset is genuinely additional and not double-counted.

High Integrity vs Low Integrity

Not all credits are equal. The voluntary carbon market spans a wide spectrum of quality, and the difference matters for your business's reputation and regulatory standing.

✓ High Integrity

What Fathom Sources

  • ✓ Third-party verified under rigorous standards (Verra VCS, Gold Standard)
  • ✓ Additional — the project wouldn't happen without carbon finance
  • ✓ Permanent or with robust risk buffers for reversal
  • ✓ Publicly traceable in a recognised registry
  • ✓ Community and biodiversity co-benefits documented
  • ✓ Not double-counted or double-sold
⚠ Low Integrity — Red Flags

What to Avoid

  • ✗ Self-certified with no independent verification
  • ✗ Based on avoided emissions that were never at risk
  • ✗ No permanence guarantee — forests felled, projects abandoned
  • ✗ No traceable retirement in a public registry
  • ✗ Suspiciously cheap with no explanation
  • ✗ Projects that harm local communities

Why Bundled Portfolios Outperform Single Credits

Just as a well-managed investment portfolio spreads risk across asset classes, a bundled carbon portfolio spreads your offset across different project types, geographies, and methodologies. No single project type is perfect — forestry faces wildfire risk, soil carbon can reverse, renewable energy becomes less additional as grids decarbonise. By blending project types, you benefit from the co-impacts of each while reducing exposure to the specific risks of any one. Our curated portfolios are built with this diversification logic from the start.

The Case for Action

Why Go Carbon Neutral?

Carbon neutrality isn't just good for the planet — it's increasingly essential for business. Here's why forward-thinking SMEs are acting now rather than waiting for regulation to force their hand.

🏗️

Become Attractive in Supply Chains

Large corporations face growing pressure to decarbonise their entire supply chains. When a multinational reviews suppliers, carbon neutrality can be the deciding factor. SMEs with verified carbon credentials are far more likely to win and retain contracts with major buyers.

🤝

Build Consumer Trust

Third-party verified carbon neutrality gives your brand something tangible — a credible, independent signal that you take sustainability seriously. Unlike marketing claims, a verified certificate is backed by evidence, deepening loyalty and attracting values-aligned customers.

⚖️

Get Ahead of Regulation

The UK, EU, and other major markets are rolling out mandatory Scope 3 disclosure requirements, supply chain due diligence laws, and carbon border adjustment mechanisms. Businesses that act voluntarily today will find compliance far less disruptive — and far less costly.

💰

Unlock Financial Benefits

Going carbon neutral often reveals operational cost savings — energy efficiency, waste reduction, and leaner supply chains frequently pay for themselves. Many lenders and insurers are beginning to offer preferential terms to businesses with verified sustainability credentials.

It's the Right Thing to Do

Beyond strategy and economics, there's a more fundamental reason: we are in a climate emergency, and every tonne of carbon that doesn't enter the atmosphere matters. As a business, you have the power to make a meaningful difference — not just by offsetting your own footprint, but by demonstrating to your industry, your customers, and your community that responsible business is possible. The most successful brands of the next decade will be those that led, not those that followed.

Getting Started

Carbon neutrality means that the total greenhouse gas emissions associated with your business — across Scope 1 (direct), Scope 2 (purchased energy), and Scope 3 (value chain) — are balanced by verified carbon removals or reductions elsewhere. In practice: measure your footprint, reduce emissions where possible, offset the remainder with verified credits retired in your name.

It's important to note that carbon neutrality doesn't mean zero emissions. It's an accounting balance — every tonne you emit is matched by a verified tonne removed or avoided somewhere else. It is a recognised, credible milestone on the path to deeper decarbonisation.

Carbon Credits & Offsets

There has been legitimate scrutiny of the carbon credit market, and some credits — particularly older forestry credits and poorly evidenced projects — have rightly faced criticism. However, high-integrity credits verified under Verra's Verified Carbon Standard or the Gold Standard represent genuine, independently audited emission reductions.

Greenwashing involves vague claims without evidence. Verified carbon credits involve independent measurement, third-party audit, public registry retirement, and permanent record-keeping. When you offset with Fathom, every tonne is publicly traceable and permanently retired in your name.

Additionality is the principle that a carbon credit should only represent an emission reduction that wouldn't have happened without the revenue from that credit — the carbon finance has to be the reason the project exists. If a wind farm was already commercially viable without carbon revenue, purchasing credits from it doesn't cause any additional carbon to be reduced. All projects on the Fathom platform are independently assessed for additionality as part of their certification process.

Every credit we source is registered in a public registry — either the Verra registry or the Gold Standard Impact Registry. When we retire a credit on your behalf, it is permanently cancelled with your organisation's name recorded. It can never be sold to another buyer or used again. We provide you with a retirement certificate including the registry reference number, project details, vintage year, and tonnes retired — independently verifiable at any time.
