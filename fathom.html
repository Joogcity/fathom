The Case for Carbon Neutrality

Why Go Carbon Neutral?

Carbon neutrality isn't just good for the planet — it's increasingly essential for business. Here's why forward-thinking SMEs are acting now.

🏗️

Become Attractive in Supply Chains

Large corporations are under growing pressure from regulators and investors to decarbonise their entire supply chains — not just their own operations. When a multinational reviews suppliers, carbon neutrality can be the deciding factor. SMEs that can demonstrate verified carbon credentials are far more likely to win and retain contracts with major buyers who have their own Net Zero commitments to meet.

🤝

Build Consumer Trust

Today's consumers are more environmentally conscious than any previous generation, and they're willing to back it up with purchasing decisions. Third-party verified carbon neutrality gives your brand something tangible — a credible, independent signal that you take sustainability seriously. Unlike marketing claims, a verified carbon neutral certificate is backed by evidence, deepening loyalty among existing customers and attracting new ones who align with your values.

⚖️

Get Ahead of Regulation

Carbon reporting requirements are expanding rapidly. The UK, EU, and other major markets are rolling out mandatory Scope 3 disclosure requirements, supply chain due diligence laws, and carbon border adjustment mechanisms. Businesses that act voluntarily today will find compliance far less disruptive and costly than those scrambling to catch up. Starting your carbon journey now builds the internal processes and data infrastructure you'll need regardless.

💰

Unlock Financial Benefits

Going carbon neutral often reveals operational cost savings — energy efficiency measures, waste reduction, and leaner supply chains frequently pay for themselves. Beyond operations, many lenders and insurers are beginning to offer preferential terms to businesses with verified sustainability credentials. ESG-aligned investment is growing, and carbon neutral SMEs are better positioned to access this capital as it becomes more mainstream.

🌍

It's Simply the Right Thing to Do

Beyond strategy and economics, there's a more fundamental reason: we are in a climate emergency, and every tonne of carbon that doesn't enter the atmosphere matters. As a business, you have the power to make a meaningful difference — not just by offsetting your own footprint, but by demonstrating to your industry, your customers, and your community that responsible business is possible. The most successful brands of the next decade will be those that led, not those that followed.

Understanding Carbon Credits

What Are Carbon Credits?

Not all carbon credits are equal. Here's how they work, how we source them, and why the difference between high and low integrity credits matters for your business.

How Carbon Credits Work

A carbon credit represents the verified removal or prevention of one tonne of CO₂ equivalent from entering the atmosphere. When your business purchases and retires a credit, you can legitimately claim an offset against that tonne of emissions in your own footprint.

Credits are generated by projects that either capture carbon (like reforestation or soil sequestration) or prevent emissions that would otherwise occur (like protecting threatened forests or replacing fossil fuels with renewables). Each project is independently audited and verified against internationally recognised standards before any credits are issued.

Retirement is the key step: once a credit is retired in a public registry on your behalf, it can never be sold or used again — ensuring your offset is genuinely additional and not double-counted.

High Integrity vs Low Integrity Credits

The voluntary carbon market spans a wide spectrum of quality. Understanding the difference protects your business from reputational risk.

✓ High Integrity

What Fathom Sources

  • ✓ Third-party verified under rigorous standards (VCS, Gold Standard)
  • ✓ Additional — the project wouldn't happen without carbon finance
  • ✓ Permanent or with robust risk buffers for reversal
  • ✓ Measurable and transparent emission reductions
  • ✓ Community and biodiversity co-benefits documented
  • ✓ Publicly traceable in a recognised registry
  • ✓ Not double-counted or double-sold
⚠ Low Integrity — Red Flags

What to Avoid

  • ✗ Self-certified with no independent verification
  • ✗ Based on avoided emissions that were never at risk
  • ✗ No permanence guarantee — forests felled, projects abandoned
  • ✗ Opaque methodology or unavailable project documentation
  • ✗ No traceable retirement in a public registry
  • ✗ Suspiciously cheap without explanation
  • ✗ Projects that harm local communities

Why Bundling Credits Improves Your Portfolio

Just as a well-managed investment portfolio spreads risk across different asset classes, a bundled carbon credit portfolio spreads your offset across different project types, geographies, and methodologies.

No single project type is perfect. Forestry projects face wildfire and deforestation risk. Agricultural soil carbon sequestration is highly measurable but can reverse. Renewable energy projects are permanent but may become less additional as clean energy grids expand.

By blending project types — say, reforestation, soil carbon, and clean cookstoves — you benefit from the co-impacts of each while reducing exposure to the specific risks of any one. Our curated portfolios are designed with this diversification logic built in from the start.

Getting Started

Carbon neutrality means that the total greenhouse gas emissions associated with your business — measured across Scope 1 (direct), Scope 2 (purchased energy), and Scope 3 (value chain) — are balanced by verified carbon removals or reductions elsewhere. In practice, this involves measuring your footprint, reducing emissions where possible, and offsetting the remainder through verified carbon credits that are retired in your name.

It's important to note that carbon neutrality doesn't mean zero emissions. Rather, it's an accounting balance: every tonne you emit is matched by a verified tonne removed or avoided somewhere else. It is a recognised, credible milestone on the path to deeper decarbonisation.

We believe that the cost of measurement should never be a barrier to sustainability action. SMEs are often priced out of the carbon consultancy market by large advisory firms. By providing GHG inventories and reduction strategies at no charge, we lower the barrier to entry and help more businesses understand and take ownership of their footprint.

Our business model is built on carbon credit sales. When you choose to offset through us, we earn a margin on the credits — but only if they deliver genuine value and you trust us enough to come back. This aligns our incentives with yours: we want your footprint measurement to be accurate, your reduction strategy to be effective, and your offset purchase to be meaningful.

For most SMEs, a comprehensive Scope 1, 2, and 3 inventory takes between two and four weeks from the point we receive your data. The timeline depends largely on how readily available your business data is — energy bills, supplier information, freight data, and so on.

We'll guide you through exactly what we need and make the data collection process as straightforward as possible. For businesses that are highly data-ready, we've completed inventories in under two weeks. For more complex supply chains, it can take a little longer, but we'll keep you informed at every stage.

Carbon Credits & Offsets

There has been legitimate scrutiny of the carbon credit market, and some credits — particularly older forestry credits and poorly evidenced projects — have rightly faced criticism. However, high-integrity carbon credits, sourced from projects verified under rigorous international standards like Verra's Verified Carbon Standard or the Gold Standard, represent genuine, independently audited emission reductions.

The distinction matters: greenwashing typically involves vague claims without evidence. Verified carbon credits involve independent measurement, third-party audit, public registry retirement, and permanent record-keeping. When you offset with Fathom, every tonne is publicly traceable and permanently retired in your name. We only work with projects that meet the highest standards of additionality, permanence, and measurability.

Additionality is the principle that a carbon credit should only represent an emission reduction that wouldn't have happened without the revenue from that credit. In other words, the carbon finance has to be the reason the project exists.

This matters because if a project would have happened anyway — say, a wind farm that was already commercially viable without carbon revenue — then purchasing credits from it doesn't actually cause any additional carbon to be reduced. Low-integrity credit schemes often fail this test. All projects on the Fathom platform are independently assessed for additionality as part of their certification process.

Every credit we source is registered and tracked in a public registry — either the Verra registry or the Gold Standard Impact Registry. When we retire a credit on your behalf, it is permanently cancelled with your organisation's name recorded. This means it can never be sold to another buyer or used again.

We provide you with a retirement certificate that includes the registry reference number, project details, vintage year, and the tonnes retired. You can independently verify this in the public registry at any time. Transparency and public auditability are the primary safeguards against double-counting in the voluntary carbon market.

Carbon offsets is a broad term that covers both emission avoidance projects (like protecting a threatened forest from being cleared, or replacing a dirty fuel source with clean energy) and carbon removal projects (like reforestation, soil carbon sequestration, or direct air capture). Carbon removal specifically refers to actively pulling CO₂ out of the atmosphere.

Our portfolios include a blend of both. Avoidance projects are often more cost-effective and have strong community co-benefits. Removal projects — particularly nature-based ones like reforestation — provide the most direct climate benefit. As the market matures and removal technologies scale, we anticipate shifting our portfolios toward a higher proportion of removal credits.
